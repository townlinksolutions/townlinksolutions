Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common insurance and investment questions

Term insurance is pure life insurance that pays a lump-sum amount to your family if you pass away during the policy period. It has no maturity benefit but offers the highest coverage at the lowest premium. If you have dependents (family, parents, loans), you almost certainly need it.

Regular life insurance (endowment/ULIP) combines insurance + savings/investment. Term insurance is insurance-only — no savings component. Term is significantly cheaper (same coverage costs 10x less), making it ideal for pure protection. Most financial planners recommend term + separate investment rather than bundled products.

For health claims: hospitalisation bills, discharge summary, doctor's prescription, and policy documents. For life claims: death certificate, original policy, claimant's ID/bank details. Requirements vary by insurer — we help you prepare the exact set needed for your specific claim.

Don't give up — many rejections are due to documentation errors, not genuine policy exclusions. Steps: (1) Get the rejection reason in writing. (2) Contact us — we'll review the reason. (3) If valid grounds exist, we assist with the re-appeal process. You can also escalate to IRDAI if the insurer is unresponsive.

SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) lets you invest a fixed amount monthly into mutual funds — starting from as low as ₹500/month. You benefit from rupee-cost averaging (buying more units when markets fall, fewer when they rise) and compounding over time. It's one of the most accessible ways to build long-term wealth.
