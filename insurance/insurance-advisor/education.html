Insurance Fundamentals

Understanding the essentials of insurance coverage and protection

What is Insurance?

Insurance is a financial protection mechanism where you pay periodic premiums in exchange for coverage against specified risks. It provides peace of mind and financial security for you and your family.

Key Components:

Premium

Regular payment made to maintain your insurance policy

Coverage

The maximum amount the insurer will pay out for a claim

Claim

Request for payment based on policy terms and conditions

Policy

The formal document outlining coverage terms and conditions

Types of Insurance

Different insurance products address various financial needs. Understanding each type helps you make informed decisions about your protection strategy.

Available Coverage Options:

Life Insurance:

Provides financial protection to your beneficiaries in case of death

Health Insurance:

Covers medical expenses for hospitalization and treatment

Motor Insurance:

Mandatory coverage for vehicles against damage and liability

Travel Insurance:

Protection against unexpected expenses during travel

Business Insurance:

Comprehensive coverage for business assets and operations

Calculating Your Needs

Determining appropriate coverage levels ensures adequate financial protection. Use industry guidelines and personal circumstances as reference points.

Coverage Guidelines:

Life Insurance Recommendation:

Annual Income × 10 = Suggested Coverage

Example: ₹5L income requires ₹50L coverage
Health Insurance Coverage:

₹5-10 Lakhs minimum per individual

Family of four: ₹20-40L combined coverage
Emergency Reserve:

Maintain 6-12 months of expenses separately

Critical financial safety net

Policy Terminology

Insurance policies contain specific terms that define coverage scope, conditions, and limitations. Familiarity with these terms helps you understand your protection level.

Important Terms:

Deductible

Your responsibility before insurance coverage begins

Co-insurance

Cost sharing arrangement between you and the insurer

Exclusion

Specified circumstances or conditions not covered by policy

Waiting Period

Timeframe before certain benefits become available

Riders

Optional add-ons for enhanced or specialized coverage

Renewal & Continuation

Continue your insurance coverage by renewing policies before expiration. Understanding renewal terms and options ensures uninterrupted protection.

Renewal Information:

Policy Renewal

Continue coverage by paying updated premiums before policy expiration date

Grace Period

Usually 30-45 days allowed after premium due date to maintain coverage without penalty

Premium Changes

Renewal premiums may change based on age, claims history, and policy terms

Continuous Coverage

Renew on time to avoid coverage gaps and maintain benefit continuity

Policy Surrender & Lapse

Understand the implications of surrendering a policy or allowing it to lapse. Both options have financial and coverage consequences to consider.

Surrender and Lapse Terms:

Policy Surrender

Voluntarily exit policy after initial period - may receive surrender value based on accumulated benefits

Surrender Value

Amount payable when you surrender the policy - typically lower than total premiums paid

Policy Lapse

Coverage becomes invalid if premium not paid within grace period - no protection from lapsed date

Revival Option

Some policies can be revived within specified period by paying outstanding premiums plus penalties

Important Note

Avoid surrendering early - you may lose significant portion of premiums paid and tax benefits received

Tax Benefits of Insurance

Maximize your tax savings while protecting your family

1

Section 80C - Life Insurance Premiums

Benefit: Deduction of life insurance premiums up to ₹1.5 lakhs per financial year

Eligible Policies: Life insurance, term insurance, endowment policies

How to Claim: Include premium receipts in your ITR (Income Tax Return) under Section 80C

Note: The deduction is limited to 10% of the policy sum assured or premium paid, whichever is lower

2

Section 80D - Health Insurance Premiums

Benefit: Deduction for health insurance premiums paid for yourself and family

Maximum Deduction:

  • For self & spouse: ₹75,000
  • For parents (age < 60): ₹75,000
  • For parents (age ≥ 60): ₹1,00,000

Eligible: Medical insurance, critical illness policies, Medicare

3

Section 80DDB - Treatment Expenses

Benefit: Deduction for treatment of serious ailments not covered by insurance

Maximum Deduction:

  • Individual (age < 60): ₹1,00,000
  • Senior citizen (age ≥ 60): ₹1,50,000

Requirements: Medical certification required; no deduction if covered by insurance

4

Insurance Claim - Tax Free Receipt

Important: Insurance claim payouts are generally tax-free under Section 10(10D)

Life Insurance Benefits: Death benefits, maturity proceeds, surrender value (in most cases)

Health Insurance: Claim amounts received are entirely tax-free

Exception: Some surrender gains may be taxable if policy is old or surrendered early
